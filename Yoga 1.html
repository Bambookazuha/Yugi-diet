Yoga



How can doing yoga help you?

Yoga benefits extend far beyond physical fitness, encompassing mental and emotional well-being. It enhances flexibility, strength, and balance while promoting mindfulness and reducing stress. Yoga improves posture, alleviates chronic pain, boosts immunity, and fosters self-awareness and personal growth. It also enhances mental clarity, concentration, and sleep quality, ultimately offering a holistic approach to wellness that empowers individuals to lead healthier, more fulfilling lives.

For Intermediate

As an intermediate practitioner, you have a good foundation to explore various types of yoga. Here are a few options you might consider.




Ashtanga yoga

Ashtanga is a structured and physically demanding style that follows a specific sequence of poses. It's ideal for those seeking a disciplined practice with a focus on breath and movement coordination.




Iyegar yoga

This style emphasizes precise alignment and the use of props to support and enhance each pose. It's excellent for developing strength, stability, and body awareness, making it well-suited for intermediate practitioners looking to refine their alignment.




Power yoga

In this 20-minute full-body power flow, we will regulate the nervous system with breath and target all major muscle groups to increase strength. Align your joints for stability, and tap into the power of your core muscles for empowered support.




For Advanced

Advanced yoga practice involves a deeper exploration of postures (asanas), breath control (pranayama), and meditation. Here's a sequence suitable for advanced practitioners:




Sun Salutations(Surya Namaskar)

Begin with several rounds of dynamic sun salutations to warm up the body and synchronize breath with movement. Arm Balances: Include challenging arm balances like Crow Pose (Bakasana), Side Crow (Parsva Bakasana), and Eight-Angle Pose (Astavakrasana) to build strength and balance.




Inversions

Practice advanced inversions such as Headstand (Sirsasana), Handstand (Adho Mukha Vrksasana), and Forearm Stand (Pincha Mayurasana) to develop focus, stability, and confidence.




Pranayama

Dedicate time to advanced pranayama techniques such as Kapalabhati (Skull Shining Breath), Nadi Shodhana (Alternate Nostril Breathing), and Bhastrika (Bellows Breath) to deepen breath awareness and control.
